## 2. COURSE BREAKDOWN - MILE BY MILE SUFFERING ZONES

### **Mile 0-11: The D Hill Mud Trap**
- **D Hill** at mile 10 - hasn't been used since 2015 because it becomes a mud pit
- In 2023, this section created "absolute madness" with peanut butter mud
- Riders went from 50-60 km/h downhill to walking/running with bikes
- Some walked 4+ miles carrying bikes
- 2023: Created 8+ miles of chaos, ended races in the first hour
- Nathan Haas: "It was absolute madness. We went in like, maybe 50-60 km/h after a downhill and then it was just guys running with bikes."

### **Mile 25: Kaw Reserve Road**
- First major technical test after fast start
- Rugged double-track with sharp rocks that challenge tire choices

### **Mile 40: First Water Oasis (Eskridge)**
- Technical section around mile 40 with steep climbs/descents
- Mechanical here can knock you out of contention for elite field
- Water only - NO support crew allowed

### **Mile 42-45.5: Divide Road - "The Relentless Stretch"**
- Begins technical, bumpy gravel that keeps riders alert
- Mile 45.5 = "one of the hardest sections of the entire route"
- Relentless rough terrain, more rugged than previous years
- Cumulative impact of chunky/loose-over-hard gravel
- Rutted descents make course "super difficult"

### **Mile 65: Prairie View Road Climb**
- New section added in 2024 (not used in 2021/2019 north routes)
- Series of rollers that "sap energy"
- "Meat and potatoes" of the north section climbing
- Most climbing in first half before Alma checkpoint

### **Mile 70: CHECKPOINT 1 - ALMA**
- First official checkpoint with crew support
- 3:00 PM cutoff
- Chaos: "People yelling, music blasting from every tent, adrenaline through the roof" - Lauren De Crescenzo
- Elite strategy: Some don't stop (grab bottles on the fly)
- Amateur strategy: 2-3 minute stops typical

### **Mile 70-88: Gradual Northern Climb**
- Gradual but continuous climbing
- Northernmost section of route
- Exposed terrain

### **Mile 104: Little Egypt - "The Storied Ascent"**
- Steep ascent over chunky rocks
- Historically: Launch pad for race-winning attacks
- Has smoothed out in recent years but still rough
- Flats common here, causing chaos and chase groups
- One rider: "rough, but not as bad compared to Divide Road"

### **Mile 112: Second Water Oasis (Alta Vista)**
- Water only - NO support crew
- Late in 2023, this station RAN OUT OF WATER during heat

### **Mile 131: Des Moines Road**
- Chunky gravel, constant ups and downs
- "Rivaled Little Egypt" in difficulty
- Cumulative fatigue setting in

### **Mile 148: CHECKPOINT 2 - COUNCIL GROVE**
- Council Grove High School, 129 Hockaday St
- Last support crew opportunity
- 11:00 PM cutoff
- Riders "weather beaten and torn" by this point
- Flint Hills Nature Trail = flat cruise into town
- Final resupply before 52-mile push to finish

### **Mile 148-177: "The Block Headwind Section"**
- Checkpoint 2 to 3 = hardest mental effort
- Longer distance, block headwind, temps approach 90°F
- Groups down to 10-15 riders by this point
- If large group leaves Council Grove together, likely stays intact until mile 177

### **Mile 175: Lake Kahola Climb - "The Last Hurdle"**
- **Longest climb of the entire day**
- Exposed, windswept ramp
- Headwinds "tend to hang out" on this long open stretch
- Wind "can create havoc"
- Half-mile Kahola Hill
- Beautiful lake community provides mental boost (boaters cheering)

### **Mile 177-202: The Final Push**
- Relatively flat after Kahola
- Mile 191: Neutral water stop at Americus (elites accelerate through)
- Mile 198-ish: Enter Emporia city limits
- **Highland Hill**: Final punchy tarmac climb along Emporia State University
- Turn onto Merchant Street, then 12th Avenue for 2 blocks
- Final straightaway on Commercial Street to finish

## 3. DNF DATA - THE BRUTAL TRUTH

### DNF Rates by Year

**2023 - THE MUD YEAR (Record DNFs)**
- General Field 200: 654 out of 1,154 finished = **43% DNF rate**
- Elite Women: 1/3 did not finish (57 starters)
- Elite Men: 1/4 dropped out (116 starters)
- **Overall**: 900 total abandons across all distances = 23% of all participants
- **550% increase** in abandons over 2022
- Winning time 44 minutes slower than 2022 (10:06:02 vs 2022's fast times)

**2022 - THE FAST YEAR**
- 1,109 out of 1,233 finished = **10% DNF rate**
- Cool, cloudy conditions (14-22°C / 57-72°F)
- Fastest finish on record until 2024

**2021**
- Similar **19% abandonment rate** (north route, similar to 2023)

**2009 - THE HEAT YEAR (Historic Low Finish Rate)**
- Winner barely beat the sun despite being "a great athlete"
- Finish time: well over 14 hours
- **Finish rate: Only 14%** = 86% DNF RATE
- "When the real feel is over 100 the temperature is crushing" - organizers

**2024**
- 902 riders finished the 200
- No specific DNF percentage published, but mechanicals/heat/crashes were factors

### Primary DNF Causes (In Order of Frequency)

**1. MECHANICALS (Most Common)**
- Tire punctures = #1 mechanical issue
- 2016 "Derailleur Armageddon": 20% of field abandoned at mile 6 - mud tore off derailleurs into rear wheels
- Broken derailleur cables
- Broken spokes destroying derailleurs
- Frame cracks from peanut butter mud
- Barbed wire encounters shredding tires
- Broken derailleur hangers

Specific Examples:
- 2022 winner Ivar Slik (2023): Broken rear derailleur cable at 20km, rode final 310km in one gear (11t), finished 36th
- Lauren De Crescenzo (2023): Ran over barbed wire, shredded tires, DNF after 9+ hours
- Nathan Haas (2023): Walked 8 miles carrying bike through mud, DNF
- 2024: Rob Britton, Brennan Wertz, Greg Van Avermaet all suffered early mechanicals

**2. HEAT & DEHYDRATION**
- "If the flint or mud doesn't get you, the heat will"
- Athletes vomiting from heat and dehydration
- Neutral aid station running out of water (2023, mile 112)
- Heat exhaustion causing withdrawals
- Organizer: "Mud is doable; heat can really really hurt you"

**3. TIME CUTOFFS**
- Missing checkpoint cutoffs eliminates riders
- Based on 10 mph average = must maintain pace throughout

**4. CRASHES & INJURIES**
- Back spasms (Payson McElveen, 2023)
- Crashes in muddy sections from accordioning peloton
- Post-mud crash at 137km (Justine Barrow, 2023)

**5. MENTAL BREAKDOWNS**
- "Gut revolts, mental breakdowns" common
- Multiple "dark places" over 12-20 hour efforts
- Running out of nutrition/hydration leading to despair

**6. MUD (Specific Years)**
- 2015 and 2023 = peanut butter mud years
- Bikes become unrideable, wheels won't turn
- Derailleurs ripped off
- Walking for miles = race over for competitive placing

## 5. EQUIPMENT FAILURES - WHAT ACTUALLY BREAKS

### Common Mechanical Failures (From Most to Least Common)

**1. Tire Punctures & Sidewall Cuts**
- "Most common mechanical riders experience"
- Sharp flint gravel is notorious
- "Rocks are razor sharp, super chunky, have tendency to move underneath you"
- Large rock hit by rider in front sends it into your path
- Ted King had tire problems every Unbound before switching to Rene Herse tires (still won twice)
- 2023: Barbed wire encounter shredding tires

**2. Derailleur Destruction**
- 2016: 20% of field lost derailleurs at mile 6 (mud threw them into rear wheels)
- Mud clogs, rips off derailleurs
- SRAM drivetrains held up better than Shimano in mud (rider observations)
- Broken cables common
- Hanger failures (Trek Boone 2016: weak hanger with small set screws)

**3. Frame Damage**
- Sticky clay mud "cracked frames and caused all-around carnage"
- 2023: Multiple broken frames from peanut butter mud

**4. Broken Spokes**
- Can destroy derailleurs when they go into mechanism

**5. Chain Issues**
- Mud ejects chains from chainrings
- Broken chains possible on rough terrain

### Equipment That Saves Races

**Tire Setup (Critical)**
- **Size**: 40-45mm minimum, 45mm+ recommended for north route
- Lachlan Morton won 2024 with 2.1" front / 44mm rear
- **Pressure**: Wide range depending on setup
  - With inserts: 27 psi (CushCore), 18/19 psi front/rear (SILCA testing)
  - Without inserts: 38-45 psi typical
  - Ian Boswell: 40-45 psi on 42mm Pathfinders
  - Alison Tetrick: 38-40 psi on 42mm Pathfinders
- **Inserts**: CushCore Gravel (110g) most popular, allows lower pressure
  - Tubolight (33g) lighter but less confidence
  - 50mm+ tires: Inserts optional due to air volume
- **Tubeless**: Essential. Extra sealant recommended (OrangeSeal)
- **Casing**: Endurance Plus or similar puncture protection mandatory

**Repair Kit Essentials**
- Dynaplug tire plug kit (gold standard)
- 2 spare tubes (TPU tubes like Schwalbe Aerothan = lighter)
- Multi-tool with chain breaker
- Spare derailleur hanger
- Gorilla tape wrapped around pump
- Zip ties
- Ability to go single-speed if derailleur breaks

**What Doesn't Work**
- Behind-saddle bottles: "A guy tried it, missing 2 bottles a few miles in"
- Light/fragile components
- Narrow tires (<40mm)
- Aero bars (banned for elite riders)

**#1 - Going Out Too Hard (UNANIMOUS)**
- "The biggest mistake almost EVERY racer makes, even pros"
- "First 90 minutes are decisive. Prepare to ride hard until race thins out after those 90 minutes"
- One rider: "I went out too hard trying to stick with fast riders. After mile 80, I was completely blown up"
- Passing in wrong spots wastes energy: "Should have hit gravel in top 25. Instead stuck in middle passing slow riders in tight quarters"
- "Passing on straight sections and rough gravel when should have been passing in corners"

**#2 - Hydration/Nutrition Failures**
- "Completely ran out of water and carb mix after mile 130. BIGGEST MISTAKE"
- "At neutral water stop, only refilled one bottle and sprayed it all on neck instead of drinking. Should have taken extra minute to fill second bottle"

- "If you get behind on hydration and nutrition it's over"

**#3 - Inadequate Heat Preparation**
- Not training in heat if from cooler climate
- Underestimating heat impact

- Not going "bullet proof with tires"
- Insufficient tire pressure planning
- Not testing nutrition/hydration in training

- "You can't grit your way through a race-ending mechanical"

- "Most prestigious and high-profile gravel race on global calendar"
- "The Kona of gravel racing"
- Started 2006 with 34 riders (18 finishers), now 5,000+ riders, 25,000+ people descend on Emporia
- "More inclusive than road racing; basically anyone can sign up and race alongside elites"
- "Refreshingly mass participation, like a marathon, rather than exclusive like WorldTour"

**The Challenge:**
- "Built to be epic"
- "Built as antidote to what existed, offering all-day, all-consuming, self-supported adventure in beautiful and barren Flint Hills"
- "Good we get hard one every couple years to remind people this event is not easy"

**Veteran Defense:**
- Organizers: "Want everyone to have shot to make it to finish, but don't have duty for everyone to make it there and nothing on course was unpassable"
- "A celebration of tenacity"

- After 43% DNF rate, serious debate about whether "epicness taken too far"

**The Dirt:**

**What Marketing Doesn't Tell You:**

## 8. TRAINING IMPLICATIONS - WHAT CATCHES PEOPLE OFF GUARD

### Physical Demands That Surprise People

**Duration Above All:**
- "For upwards of 15 or more consecutive hours, riders need to operate anywhere from 50-70% of threshold"
- Not just fitness - calluses, equipment testing, mental stamina
- Even without completing full 200 in training, need capability for 120-140 mile rides (60-70% race distance)
- Rule: "If capable of completing 60% goal race distance without needing several days off after, you can make it to finish. 70% to be extra safe"

**The First 90 Minutes:**
- Decisive period
- Prepare to ride hard until race thins out
- Determines group you're with for next 100+ miles

**Hours 8-12:**
- "Strong riders slowly disintegrate"
- Multiple dark periods
- "You're going to crack. Then again, and probably again."

**The Cumulative Effect:**
- Not one hard section - it's Divide Road + Prairie View rollers + Little Egypt + heat + headwind + Des Moines Road + Kahola
- "Relentless onslaught of rough, rocky paths"
- "Constant chunky/loose-over-hard gravel and rutted descents"

### Heat Adaptation Requirements (Often Overlooked)

**Why It Matters:**
- "Heat that scares the shit out of me" - race director
- 2009: 86% DNF rate in extreme heat
- Temps can exceed 100°F (38°C)
- Aid stations can run out of water
- Vomiting from dehydration common

**Training Protocol:**
- If recently heat-adapted: 3-4 sessions, start 10 days before race
- If NOT recently heat-adapted: At least 10 sessions within 14 days, starting 21 days before race
- Ideal: 2-5 weeks focused heat training block to develop foundation
- Methods:
  - Sauna training (Whitney Allison: 2 weeks, "huge benefits")
  - Travel to warm locations (California, Arizona)
  - Long rides in heat to test hydration/fueling
  - "Long rides in cold don't help much"

**What Unprepared Riders Experience:**
- "How poorly acclimated I was to heat and how bad my fueling was"
- Drifting "to back of group several times to vomit in private before moving back up"
- Running out of liquids with 70+ miles to go
- Heat exhaustion withdrawals

### Fueling Challenges (Bigger Than Expected)

**Aid Station Gaps:**
- Checkpoints every 45-90 miles (general rule)
- Specific gaps:
  - Start to Mile 70 (Alma): 70 miles
  - Mile 70 to Mile 148 (Council Grove): 78 miles
  - Mile 148 to Finish: 54 miles
- Water oasis at miles 40 and 112 (water ONLY)
- "Must carry water and food, fix own tires and bikes"
- "Extremely remote area. Rarely pass through towns. No convenience stores between checkpoints"

**The Reality:**
- "Unbound is eating competition, but it's true!"
- One rider: "Food and fluid in Racing Hydration Vest, food in top tube bag, drink mix in bottles. Resupplying all at aid stations"
- Must carry supplies to travel full checkpoint distances
- "Three checkpoints, each about 50 miles apart. Hydration and nutrition strategy between each is different"
- Early morning: Cool temps, shorter first leg = less hydration needed
- Mid-day: Heat peaks, longer sections, higher demands
- Late day/night: Fatigue affects appetite/GI system

**Common Failures:**
- Only refilling one bottle at water stop
- Spraying water on neck instead of drinking
- Not eating enough early (hard to catch up later)
- GI distress from heat/prolonged effort
- Eating only gels/junk food (Doritos, Slim Jims)
- Not conditioning stomach to accept 100g carbs/hour on long days

### Durability Requirements (Underestimated)

**Mechanical Skills:**
- "Must know how to fix broken chain, fix flat, patch tire with boot, change to single-speed if derailleur breaks, replace derailleur hanger"
- "Need to do this QUICKLY"
- Practice until confident
- Can't rely on neutral support in remote areas

**Equipment Durability:**
- "Fastest tire is one with air in it"
- Puncture protection > rolling resistance
- "Bullet proof tires" essential
- Inserts save races
- Extra sealant required
- Spare everything: tubes, hangers, chain links

**Mental Durability:**
- "Everyone experiences dark moments, usually goes away if you wait long enough"
- "Multiple winds" through 12-20 hour effort
- Sleep deprivation for slower finishers (riding into darkness/night)
- Landmark trick: "Pick landmark, keep pedaling to that point, pick new one, repeat"
- "Breaking big race down makes something massive feel achievable"

**Body Durability:**
- Calluses from long days in saddle
- Back issues from rough terrain
- Hand/wrist fatigue from constant vibration
- Night riding challenges for 15-20 hour finishers
- Circadian rhythm fatigue once sun sets
- "Deep of night, when we should all be asleep" = worst hours
- "If you can keep going until daylight, generally feel better once sun comes up"

### What "Adequate Preparation" Actually Means

**Training Volume:**
- Sweet Spot I and II + Sustained Power Build recommended
- 6+ hour rides at least once per weekend
- Some weekends: Two 6+ hour rides
- Consistency > peak efforts: "1-2 hours a day, 3-6 days a week better than one 5-hour ride a week"
- "Don't necessarily need 200-mile training ride" - one experienced rider's longest was 120 miles with 5 long rides in 60-90 mile range

**Testing Everything:**
- "Very important to test hydration and fueling for long ride in heat"
- Test tire pressure for conditions
- Test all equipment under race-like stress
- Test nutrition at race intensity for 6-8 hours
- Test mechanical repairs under pressure
- Walk through whole experience mentally
- Talk through aid station experiences with crew
- Discuss crisis plans: mechanicals, crashes, spray downs

**The Preparation Gap:**
- Sven Nys (world champion) just "showed up" in 2018: DNF
- "People came unprepared and did not understand what the event was"
- "One of those events you can't just show up at start line without significant preparation"
- "Whether planning to simply complete event or get podium finish, you need to prepare for one of hardest physical challenges you will ever complete on bike"

## 9. FINISHING TIMES - REALITY CHECK

### Elite Times
- **2025 Winner (Cameron Jones)**: 8:37 (fastest ever, breaking Morton's 2024 record)
- **2024 Winner (Lachlan Morton)**: 9:11 (22 mph / 35.4 km/h average)
- **2023 Winner (Keegan Swenson)**: 10:06:02 (44 minutes slower than 2022 due to mud)
- **2023 Women's Winner (Carolin Schiff)**: 11:46
- **2006 First Winner (Dan Hughes)**: 12:58

### Competitive Amateur Times
- **Sub-12 hours**: Competitive amateur goal
- **11:58:20**: One amateur's 2024 finish (90th place non-elite, moving time 11:51:23, stopped only ~7 minutes all day)
- **12-14 hours**: Solid amateur performance
- **12:56:31**: One rider's 2022 time, improved to 11:58 in 2024

### Mid-Pack Times
- **14-17 hours**: Common for many finishers
- **16:45**: One rider's finish after starting with 14-hour pace group

### Back-of-Pack Times
- **17-20 hours**: Pushing cutoff limits
- **20+ hours**: "They always let a few squeak through"
- Riding into darkness/night inevitable for 15+ hour finishers

### What This Means
- Elite finish before dark (8-10 hours)
- Mid-pack finishes in daylight or early evening (11-14 hours)
- Slower finishers ride through sunset, darkness, possible full night
- Sleep deprivation becomes factor for 18-20 hour finishers
- Pre-dawn 5:50 AM start + 20 hour limit = 1:50 AM finish deadline

## 10. COURSE EVOLUTION & CHANGES (2019-2025)

### Course Direction Pattern
- Alternates between north and south routes from Emporia every 2 years
- North route: Used only 3 times in 19-year history (2019, 2021, 2024, 2025)
- North route characteristics: More exposed terrain, relentless rollers, sharper/more angular gravel

### Year-by-Year Changes

**2019:**
- North route
- Taxing loop north of Emporia

**2020:**
- CANCELLED due to COVID-19
- Was postponed from May 29-31 to September 10-13, then cancelled
- October 29, 2020: Name changed from "Dirty Kanza" to "Unbound Gravel"

**2021:**
- North route (2nd time in history)
- 19% abandonment rate
- Dry, warm conditions (avoided 2015 mud and 2018 heat extremes)

**2022:**
- **Southbound direction** into Greenwood and Chase counties
- Original: 204.6 miles, updated to 199.98 miles after modification
- **Last-minute changes**: Heavy rain and river flooding days prior made parts impassable
- After Eureka checkpoint: Headed east rather than south (modification)
- Result: Cool conditions (14-22°C), fastest finish on record until 2024

**2023:**
- 205.3 miles originally, 9,101 feet climbing
- Massive loop format
- **Major format/rule changes** (first since 2006):
  - Separate starts for elite men and women
  - Aero bars banned for elite riders
  - Improved rider safety measures
- **D Hill returned** at mile 10 (not used since 2015)
  - Created peanut butter mud chaos
  - Race director knew it was susceptible to mud, indicated basic reroute possible
  - Decision NOT to reroute criticized by veterans
- Result: 43% DNF rate, 550% increase in abandons over 2022

**2024:**
- **North route** (3rd time in history)
- 202.9 miles, **11,850 feet elevation gain**
  - 2.6 miles shorter than 2023
  - 2,750 MORE feet of climbing than 2023
  - 2,000 feet more than 2019 and 2021 north editions
- **New sections added:**
  - Prairie View Road climb at mile 65 (first time ever)
  - Hillier western route out of Alma
  - Volland Road climb
- **Safety improvements:**
  - Redesigned finish line with closed finish
  - Two separate chutes for different race distances
  - Response to 2023 finish line safety issues
- **Course % split**: 92% gravel, 8% tarmac
- Result: "Perhaps toughest yet" - "relentless onslaught of rough, rocky paths"

**2025:**
- **North route again** (4th time, keeping 2024 route)
- 202.4 miles, 10,122 feet elevation gain
  - Slightly less climbing than 2024 (11,850 ft)
- "Near-identical to that taken in 2024"
- 93% unpaved roads, 14.6 miles tarmac
- "Everything worked" - organizers keeping successful 2024 north course
- 100-milers share final 70 miles with 200 route

### Key Takeaway
- No two years are the same
- Route changes every 2 years (some riders familiar with terrain if raced 2 years prior)
- Weather variability makes each edition unique
- Course difficulty has generally increased (more climbing, more technical sections)
- 2024-2025 north route = current "standard" with highest technical difficulty

## 12. WHAT THE MARKETING DOESN'T TELL YOU

### The "Do Not Call Us" Policy
- Remote roads through vast prairie lands
- "Do not call us" policy for breakdowns and injuries
- You're on your own between checkpoints
- Neutral support limited

### The Lottery Reality
- Registration is free, but credit card charged only if accepted
- Can't accommodate everyone who wants to participate
- Random selection = no guarantees
- Must enter during 2-week window (Nov 1-15)
- 2 months earlier than traditional January timing (2025 change)

### The Real Costs
- $295 entry fee (2024)
- Plus processing fee, state sales tax, add-on items
- Lodging nearly impossible in Emporia
- Must book hour+ away (Topeka, etc.)
- Limited dining options night before race
- Travel costs to Kansas
- Equipment investment (tires, inserts, gear)
- "Thousands of dollars wasted" if DNF from mechanical/conditions

### The Suffering They Don't Advertise
- "Flats, mechanicals, mud, crashes, gut revolts, mental breakdowns, heat stroke, navigational errors, dropped or missed nutrition, dehydration, and injuries are just some of the stories that took athletes out of contention"
- Sleep deprivation for slower finishers
- Riding alone in darkness for hours
- Vomiting from heat/dehydration
- Walking miles carrying bike through mud
- 8-12 hours watching "strong riders slowly disintegrate"
- "Constant chunky/loose-over-hard gravel and rutted descents"

### What "Self-Supported" Really Means
- Only 2 crew-access checkpoints in 200 miles
- Must carry everything needed between checkpoints (70-78 mile gaps)
- Fix your own mechanicals or race is over
- Water oasis = water ONLY (no crew, no parts, limited nutrition)
- Aid stations can run out of water
- If you need help, you're likely DNF

### The Weather Lottery
- "Notorious for unpredictable weather"
- Could be 100°F+ or hail
- Could be peanut butter mud or perfect conditions
- Tornadoes possible
- "Must prepare bikes, bodies, minds for any conditions"
- No postponing for weather

### The D Hill Decision (2023 Controversy)
- Race director KNEW D Hill (mile 10) was susceptible to mud
- Heavy rain days before race
- Mentioned "basic reroute could be made"
- **Decided NOT to reroute**
- Result: 43% DNF rate, 8 miles of chaos in first hour
- 6-time participant: "Significantly hurt the event and the experiences of many who participated"

### The Organizer's Honest Take
- "Want everyone to have shot to make it to finish, but don't have duty for everyone to make it there"
- "People came unprepared and did not understand what the event was"
- "Heat can really really hurt you"
- "Good we get hard one every couple years to remind people this event is not easy"

### What Actually Ends Races (In Order)
1. Tire punctures / sidewall cuts
2. Derailleur destruction
3. Heat exhaustion / dehydration
4. Time cutoffs
5. Frame damage
6. Broken spokes
7. Mental breakdown
8. Crashes
9. GI distress
10. Navigation errors

### The Reality Check
- 2009: 86% DNF rate in heat
- 2023: 43% DNF rate in mud
- Even "good" years: 10-20% DNF rate
- World champions DNF
- 2-time winners have had tire problems every year before finding right setup
- "Nothing is guaranteed"
- "You can't grit your way through a race-ending mechanical"
- "Error or mechanical will quickly end your race at the front"
