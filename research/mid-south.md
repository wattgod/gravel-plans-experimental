## TERRAIN & SURFACE

**Surface breakdown:** 98-99% unpaved, 1-2% pavement

**Geographic Context:**
- Cross Timbers ecoregion
- Tribal lands: Osage, Quapaw, Kickapoo, Wichita, Kiowa Nations
- Mix of prairie and woodland

**Geological Features:**
- Red dirt from iron oxide (pinkish hue)
- Significant clay content (especially western half)
- Sandstone outcroppings
- "Sunken roadbed" sections with sandstone walls

**Road Surface:**
- Clay hardback when dry = fast, hard-pack riding
- "Not the frighteningly chunky gravel you find in the Flint Hills at Unbound Gravel"
- "Dry and dusty" when conditions cooperate
- "Hard pack" riding = slick center tread tires work well

## TIRE RECOMMENDATIONS

### DRY CONDITIONS (Most Common):
- **WTB Byway 40mm:** Slick center tread, "Efficient, with enough rubber"
- **WTB Byway 44mm:** "Better traction and more air volume for comfort"
- **Maxxis Reaver 40mm:** Full slick with tiny file tread
- **Specialized Pathfinder Pro 42-44mm:** Dual-compound (hard center, soft sides)

### WET/MUD CONDITIONS:
- **Kenda Kommando 32mm:** Cross-tire, narrower = less mud clogging in fork

### TIRE PRESSURE GUIDANCE:
- Pro Alex Howes: "High 20s PSI" for Mid South specifically
- Maxxis Reaver runner: "31-32 PSI start, by end of day 32-33 PSI"
- "Wide tire at lower pressures is your traction"
